March 2015 Andrew will be playing Alessandro in the Arched Brow Theatre Company’s staged reading of Tenure Track: The Musical at the Apollo Theater Chicago on March 9th and 10th.

January 2015 Andrew wrote and produced a humorous short, Dominique and Andrew for Ellen: The Quest to be Interns, on the length new graduates must go in order to be noticed by employers in today’s saturated job market. #dominiqueandandrewforellen

December 2014 Andrew finished shooting Ketchup and Blood, a dramatic short which will be submitted to the major film festivals.

November 2014 The Emerson College BFA Showcase will be performing at the Laurie Beechman Theatre on November 10th in New York City.

March 2014 Andrew made his Boston debut in Hello Again at Bridge Repertory Theater.

Andrew Spatafora is a Chicago born-and-raised actor, singer, and unabashed Saturday night movie-marathoner who has experience in theater, film, and commercial work. He recently earned his BFA in Musical Theatre from Emerson College in Boston. Additionally, he has studied improvisation at The Second City Training Center in Chicago, and has trained at New York University’s Steinhardt Musical Theatre Intensive.

Recently, Andrew has been seen in Bridge Repertory Theater’s production of Hello Again, and in The Vineyard Playhouse’s production of The Three Musketeers, directed by Scott Barrow (Tectonic Theater Project, NY). At Emerson, Andrew appeared in the original full production of Ryan Scott Oliver and Brett Ryback’s Darling: A New Musical, where he worked closely with Oliver and Ryback along with Grammy Award winner Kurt Deutsch (of Sh-K-Boom/Ghostlight Records) and Tony Award nominee Sheryl Kaller (Next Fall). He also has been seen in Emerson Stage productions of Starting Here, Starting Now and She Loves Me, in addition to productions of The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee, Urinetown, and Speech and Debate.

Over the years, Andrew has trained with leading professionals of the stage and screen including Broadway veteran Jim Corti (A Chorus Line, Ragtime); 10-Time Jeff Award winner Hollis Resnik; stage and screen actor Ken Cheeseman (Shutter Island, The Forger); Emerson College actor and director Scott LaFeber; director Marcia Milgrom Dodge (Ragtime); actress and director Lee Fierro (Jaws); Second City Training Center Artistic Director Matt Hovde; and NYU professors Michael Ricciardone and John Simpkins. Additionally, Andrew has worked with LA casting directors Kerry Barden (Pitch Perfect, Good Will Hunting), Scott David (Criminal Minds), and Erica Silverman (Star Trek Into Darkness).

Outside of Musical Theatre, Andrew won “Best Actor” at Campus MovieFest for his work in the short film Barnes, which subsequently was nominated for a Golden Tripod Award in Los Angeles. Andrew also has been seen as 2nd Lieutenant Wilson in the independent film Cocktail Party, directed by 4-time CINE Golden Eagles Award Recipient Regge Life (The Cosby Show), and has performed on The Cutler Majestic Stage in Boston as part of the 31st Annual EVVY Awards, under the direction of Kevin Bright (Friends). Behind the scenes, Andrew recently spent a summer working as the Development Department Intern at the Tony Award-winning Victory Gardens Theatre in Chicago.
